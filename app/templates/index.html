Platform Scope

From primary sequence to structural and functional interpretation

This platform is designed as an academic-style protein annotation portal. It starts from a submitted human protein and missense substitution, then assembles sequence statistics, residue-context analysis, AlphaFold confidence, PDB cross-references, conservation, transcript mapping, and disease-linked evidence into a single visual report.

The goal is not only to compute annotations, but to present them in an order that matches how researchers actually inspect a candidate variant: identify the protein, validate the substitution, locate it in sequence and structure, then evaluate functional and disease relevance.

Analytical Coverage

What the report is organised to show

The site is structured as an academic annotation workflow rather than a generic dashboard. Each report is grouped into sequence, structure, and evidence layers.

🧬

Sequence Annotations

Residue-level and sequence-wide properties

  • Canonical human protein resolution via UniProt
  • Missense variant parsing & HGVS normalisation
  • Wild-type vs mutant physicochemical metrics (MW, pI, GRAVY, aromaticity, instability index)
  • Protein architecture — domain & motif track
  • Evidence lollipop from curated annotations
  • Interval and site-level feature tables
  • Chromosome & exon mapping (Ensembl / NCBI)
  • Primate conservation profile
  • Phylogenetic tree view (when available)
🦴

Structural Annotations

Predicted and experimental 3D context

  • PSIPRED — secondary structure per residue (helix / strand / coil)
  • DMVFL-RSA — relative & absolute solvent accessibility
  • Integrated structural verdict at the variant site
  • Amphipathic helix detection (Eisenberg μH)
  • Mutation stability prediction (ΔΔG, BLOSUM62, volume, hydrophobicity)
  • AlphaFold2 pLDDT confidence track + AlphaMissense score
  • Experimental PDB cross-references (ranked by coverage & resolution)
  • Inline 3D molecular viewer (3Dmol.js)
📊

Disease & Function

Clinical and functional context from public databases

  • Human Protein Atlas — subcellular localisation, protein class, disease involvement, RNA expression summary
  • Open Targets Platform — disease association scores (top 20), known drugs & clinical trial phase, pathway memberships
  • Genomic location context (chromosome, transcript, exon structure)
  • MaveDB deep mutational scan evidence (when available)
  • InterPro / EBI Proteins curated annotations

Interpretation Logic

How to read the output

The report is designed for fast interpretation by moving from basic identity and location questions to higher-level structural and disease-context reasoning.

Layer A

Sequence identity and residue context

Confirm the submitted protein, variant notation, local amino-acid neighborhood, and sequence-wide metric shifts.

Layer B

Structure and confidence

Review AlphaFold confidence, predicted accessibility, secondary structure, and any experimental structure coverage.

Layer C

Biological and disease evidence

Inspect expression, known disease associations, pathway context, transcript mapping, and linked functional resources.
